Why This Matters

Basel-style liquidity requirements can create structural demand for sovereign bonds because government securities often play a central role in bank liquidity buffers. This makes regulation an important driver of bond market demand.

What This Tool Is

This is a simplified analytical visualizer designed to illustrate the directional relationship between regulation, liquidity buffers and bond demand. It is not a regulatory reporting model.

Methodology

The tool estimates government bond demand using a simplified top-down framework:

Estimated Bond Demand = Total Assets × Liquidity Buffer × HQLA Share × Government Bond Share

A stress adjustment can be applied to the liquidity buffer to simulate tighter liquidity conditions.
